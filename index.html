Twitch Has Banned Gambling!

In September 2021, Twitch banned all forms of gambling and betting on its platform, including games of chance, sports betting, and in-game skins gambling. This decision was made to help protect its users, especially minors, from the potential harm of gambling and to align with the company's policies on promoting safe and responsible use of its services. Twitch has stated that it will enforce the ban through strict moderation and has also advised users to report any instances of gambling or betting in violation of the ban.

What Is Esports

Esports stands for electronic sports and refers to competitive video gaming. It involves professional players competing against each other in organized tournaments and leagues, often for large prizes. Esports has grown significantly in popularity in recent years and has become a global phenomenon, attracting millions of fans and players from all over the world.

Future of Esports

The global Esports market was valued at over $1 billion in 2020, and it is estimated to reach over $1.79 billion by 2023.
